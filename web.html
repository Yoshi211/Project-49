Interesting Facts about Camels

  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels
  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat

Interesting Facts about Pigeons

  • They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds
  • Pigeons can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet and at an average speed of 77.6 mph

Interesting Facts about Cicadas

  • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans
  • More than 3,000 species exist
  • Their wings repel water and bacteria

Interesting Facts about Pandas

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim
  • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day
  • Prehistoric pandas lived up to 2 million years ago

Interesting Facts about Bears

  • Bears can cover short distances at speeds of up to 50 km/h
  • Their sense of smell enables them to sniff food at a distance of several kilometres
  • Bears in the wild are mainly active at dusk and at night.

Interesting Facts about Chameleons

  • They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature
  • Skin crystals enable them to change color at will
  • Chameleons can't regrow their tail
